Concina, E. (2023). Effective Music Teachers and Effective Music Teaching Today: A Systematic Review. _Education Sciences_, _13_(2), 107. https://doi.org/10.3390/educsci13020107
	This research synthesizes the main qualities of effective music education through reviewing studies related to the topic. The review identifies effective music education includes: a student-centered teaching approached, teachers that receive adequate preservice and in-service training, good level of social competence, being good performers and having good communication. An effective music educator can bring a student-centered environment and "become a learning facilitator for her or his students, encouraging them to gradually become autonomous and self-regulated with respect to their learning process".
	This source is useful for the process of creating criteria to assess the quality of Vinschool's current music educators and what aspect can they improve on for more effective music education.
Concina, E. (2015). Music Education and Effective Teaching: Perspectives from a Critical Review. *Literacy Information and Computer Education Journal (LICEJ), Volume 6, Issue 2*. https://infonomics-society.org/wp-content/uploads/licej/published-papers/volume-6-2015/Music-Education-and-Effective-Teaching.pdf
	The research outlines the influences of effective music education but also provide criticisms on the shortcomings of current research. Teacher-student relationships, the development of student's self-regulatory skills, verbal communication and training are the catalysts of effective music education mentioned in the study. The author argues that current research "need further experimental validation". In addition, the author also argues that the current elements identified in effective music education is being reviewed individually and should be analyzed in "a more wide view that can connect them into a general model of teaching".
	 The research assists in providing elements that influence effective music education. Another takeaway from the research is that these elements should be viewed together in a more systematic way rather than individually.
